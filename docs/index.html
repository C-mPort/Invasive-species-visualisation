Eastern Grey Squirrel (Sciurus carolinensis)

Species Info

Name: Eastern Grey Squirrel / Grey Squirrel

Scientific Name: Sciurus carolinensis

Native Range: Native to Eastern North America and Southeastern Canada

How they entered the UK: Were brought over in the late 1800s to populate wealthy Victorian estates and later spread across the country.

Environmental Impact: Caused localised extinctions of native red squirrels across most of England; Wales; and Northern Ireland, damage to fruit crops, eating native bird species eggs and destroying nests, stripping bark from trees which can kill trees.

Muntjac Deer (Muntiacus reevesi)

Species Info

Name: Muntjac Deer / Barking Deer / Rib-faced Deer

Scientific Name: Muntiacus reevesi

Native Range: China, Taiwan, India, and parts of Southeast Asia

How they entered the UK: Were brought over to a safari park in Woburn Abbey in the early 1900s. After a couple of years, they escaped the park and feral populations were established.

Environmental Impact: Damage to young trees, shrubs, and woodland flora caused by grazing, linked to declines in native bird species such as nightingales due to habitat alteration.

Egyptian Goose (Alopochen aegyptiaca)

Species Info

Name: Egyptian Goose / Fox Goose

Scientific Name: Alopochen aegyptiaca

Native Range: Africa, specifically the Nile valley and South Saharan desert

How they entered the UK: Were brought over in the late 1800s to populate wealthy Victorian estates and later spread across the country.

Environmental Impact: Risk of hybridisation with native geese, damage to grasslands and crops, water pollution from droppings leading to oxygen depletion and threats to aquatic life. Can also pose health risks to humans (linked to E. coli, toxoplasmosis, and avian influenza).
